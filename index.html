Sean E. Doris

Materials Chemist

Research interests: Energy storage and conversion, printed and flexible electronics, nanomaterials, surface chemistry, and related topics

Education and Experience

PARC, a Xerox company

Member of Research Staff (2018-Present), Post-doctoral Researcher (2017-2018)
As a member of the Electronic Materials and Devices Laboratory at PARC, I work to develop materials, processes, and applications for next-generation printed and flexible electronics, including printed sensors, hybrid chemical electronic systems, and systems that support energy storage and harvesting.

University of California, Berkeley

Ph.D. in Physical Chemistry (2012-2016)
As a graduate student, I developed and studied the mechanism for a new class of reactions for controlling nanocrystal surface chemistry, developed a new class of size-selective membranes for non-aqueous batteries, and studied the electrochemistry of redox-mediators for the electro-deposition of non-conductive energy storage materials. In each of these projects, I applied materials science and electrochemistry to solving applied problems in the fields of energy storage and conversion.
Co-advised by: Dr. Brett A. Helms (LBNL) and Prof. Omar Yaghi (UC Berkeley)
Selected Coursework: Statistical Mechanics, Materials Chemistry, and Surface Properties of Materials
Teaching: Organic chemistry laboratory (Chem 3AL), 3 semesters, 2012-2015

University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill

Bachelor of Science in Chemistry with Highest Distinction (2008-2012)
Advised by: Prof. Royce Murray. GPA: 3.92 cumulative, 3.95 chemistry.
Selected Coursework: Electroanalytical Chemistry, Separations, and Mechanisms of Organic and Inorganic Reactions.
Teaching: Volunteer chemistry tutor (Chemistry Resource Center), 2 semesters, 2010-2011

Skills

  • Synthesis

    Common organic, inorganic, and nanocrystal synthesis procedures, including oxygen and moisture-free atmosphere procedures (Schlenk and glove box techniques)

  • Electroanalytical techniques

    Cyclic voltammetry, chronoamperometry, rotating ring-disk electrode experiments, electrochemical impedance spectroscopy, and battery cycling (galvanostatic and potentiostatic)

  • Lab Management

    Experience setting up new lab space and infrastructure, including glove box maintenance and equipment requisition

  • Imaging

    Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM), limited exposure to Transmission Electron Microscopy (TEM) and Atomic Force Microscopy (AFM)

  • Separations

    High pressure liquid chromatography (HPLC), capillary electrophoresis (CE), and gas chromatography-mass spectrometry (GC-MS)

  • Computers and Electronics

    Diagnosing hardware and software problems, hardware configuration, and software installation/configuration. Proficient in using Python for automated data analysis and instrument control.

  • Spectroscopy

    Experienced with Inductively Coupled Plasma Atomic Emission Spectroscopy (ICP-AES), UV-Vis, FT-IR, and NMR spectroscopies (including advanced 2D NMR techniques)

  • Materials Characterization

    Thermogravimetric analysis, dynamic light scattering, zeta potential measurements, powder X-ray diffraction, Grazing Incidence Small Angle X-ray Scattering (GISAXS)
